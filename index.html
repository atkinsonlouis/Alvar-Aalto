Alvar Aalto

Finland's greatest architect

Here's a time line of Alvar Aalto's life:

  • 1898 - Born in Kuortane, Finland
  • 1916 - Completed basic education at Jyväskylä Lyceum school
  • 1916 - Enrolled to study architecture at the Helsinki University of Technology. His studies were however interrupted by the Finnish civil war, in which he fought.
  • 1920 - Travelled to Gothenburg and found work with architect Arvid Bjerke
  • 1922 - accomplished his first independent piece at the Industrial Exposition in Tampere
  • 1923 - returned to Jyväskylä, where he opened an architectural office under the name 'Alvar Aalto, Architect and Monumental Artist'
  • 1924 - Married architect Aino Marsio.
  • 1927 - Won the architecture competition for the Southwest Finland Agricultural Cooperative building and subsequently moved to Turku
  • 1933 - Aalto and his wife moved their offices to Helsinki
  • 1935 - Along with his wife Aino, Aalto designed a joint house-office in Munkkiniemi which is now a home museum
  • 1949 - His wife Aino Aalto sadly died of cancer
  • 1952 - married architect Elissa Mäkiniemi, who had been working as an assistant in his office
  • 1952 - designed and built a summer cottage, the so-called Experimental House, for himself and his new wife in Muuratsalo in Central Finland
  • 1956 - Designed an office in the same neighbourhood which is now the Alvar Aalto academy
  • 1962 - Finished the design of Finlandia Hall
  • 1976 - dies at the age of 78

Beauty is the harmony of purpose and form
